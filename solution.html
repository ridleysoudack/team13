Canopy Layer Production

The screenshots below provide a summary of the canopy layer production process as well as examples of the final canopy layer polygons.

LiDAR Point Cloud

We began with classified LiDAR data where points classied as Medium and High vegetation were isolated to create initial polygon layers.

LiDAR Misclassification

A large amount of LiDAR points were misclassified that needed to be corrected or removed. Objects such as corners of roofs, signs, streetlights, etc. were manually removed to create a clean polygon layer.

Tree Stand Segmentation

In areas of closely spaced trees, canopies would blend together and were not discernible in LiDAR data. For inventoried trees, these polgyons needed to be segmented into individual canopies which was done manually.

Final Tree Canopy Layers

Once the canopy layers were validated, various operations were performed to smooth and simplify the polygons. Data from tree inventory points were joined to the canopy polygons which was used with our growth model to 'grow' the canopy from 2017 to the present day.
