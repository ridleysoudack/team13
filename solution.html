Canopy Layer Production

The screenshots below provide a summary of the canopy layer production process as well as examples of the final canopy layer polygons.

LiDAR Point Cloud

We began with classified LiDAR data where points classied as Medium and High vegetation were isolated to create initial polygon layers.

LiDAR Misclassification

A large amount of LiDAR points were misclassified that needed to be corrected or removed. Objects such as corners of roofs, signs, streetlights, etc. were manually removed to create a clean polygon layer.

Tree Stand Segmentation

In areas of closely spaced trees, canopies would blend together and were not discernible in LiDAR data. For inventoried trees, these polgyons needed to be segmented into individual canopies which was done manually.

Final Tree Canopy Layers

Once the canopy layers were validated, various operations were performed to smooth and simplify the polygons. Data from tree inventory points were joined to the canopy polygons which was used with our growth model to 'grow' the canopy from 2017 to the present day.

Tree Growth Model

The screenshots below provide a summary of the growth model devleopment.

ArcGIS Notebook

The ArcGIS Notebook contains two parts. Part 1 consists of Setup and creating initial canopy layers. Part 2 models the amount of growth for each inventoried tree and applies a buffer distance to the canopy polygons to grow them based on the specificed parameters.

Example of Tree Growth

In this image we can see an example of the results of the growth model. The inner dashed line represents the canopy extent in 2017. The outer dashed line is the modeled growth of the canopy in 5 years to 2021.

Created Canopy (Buffers)

Canopies were created through buffering based on expected growth. In this image you can see the overlap that needed to be removed (Monique St). Light green polygons within the buffers are present day canopy.

Created Canopy (Dissolved)

Upclose image of the dissolved buffers.

Species that have reached Max Spread

When species have reached their maximum canopy spread buffers are not created. In this image there are a number of species that have reached their maximum.
